Medicinal Plants

Tulsi (Holy Basil)

Known for its medicinal properties, Tulsi is used to treat colds, coughs, and respiratory issues.

Neem

Neem is used for skin care and to improve digestion. Its antibacterial properties are highly valued.

Ashwagandha

Ashwagandha is used for its stress-relieving and energy-boosting properties.

Aloe Vera

Aloe Vera is used in skincare and for treating burns and improving digestion.

Brahmi

Brahmi is known for enhancing memory and cognitive abilities, used in Ayurvedic medicines.

Turmeric

Turmeric is an anti-inflammatory and antioxidant commonly used to improve skin and digestion.

Basil

Used in cooking and has antibacterial properties. Basil is also known for its potential anti-inflammatory effects.

Coriander

Used for its antioxidant and digestive benefits.Coriander seeds are used as a spice, and the leaves are used in salads and dishes.

Ginger

Known for its anti-nausea and anti-inflammatory properties. It is commonly used in cooking and as a remedy for nausea.

Chamomile

Used to promote relaxation and help with sleep. Chamomile tea is a popular remedy for insomnia and anxiety.

Dandelion

Used as a diuretic and to aid digestion. The leaves, roots, and flowers are used in various herbal remedies.

Echinacea

Boosts the immune system and is used to prevent colds. Echinacea is often used in herbal supplements and teas.

Fennel

Known for its digestive benefits and ability to reduce bloating. Fennel seeds are used in cooking and traditional remedies.

Hibiscus

Rich in antioxidants and used to lower blood pressure. Hibiscus tea is a popular beverage known for its tart flavor.

Jasmine

Used in aromatherapy for its calming effects and to reduce stress. Jasmine flowers are also used in perfumes.

Lavender

Used in aromatherapy for relaxation and sleep improvement. The scent is known for its calming effects.

Mint

Commonly used to aid digestion and relieve headaches. Mint leaves can be used in teas and culinary dishes.

Oregano

Contains antioxidants and is used for its anti-inflammatory effects. Oregano is commonly used in Italian cuisine and traditional medicine.

Parsley

Rich in vitamins and used for its diuretic properties. Parsley is also a common garnish and has various health benefits.

Rose

Used for skin care and in cosmetics. The petals have anti-inflammatory properties and are often used in face masks and lotions.

Rosemary

Used to improve memory and concentration, and for its antioxidant properties. Rosemary is commonly used in culinary dishes.

Sage

Known for its digestive benefits and antimicrobial properties. Sage is often used in cooking and herbal medicine.

Thyme

Has antimicrobial properties and is used in cooking and medicine. Thyme is also used in traditional remedies for respiratory conditions.

Turmeric

Contains curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. Often used in cooking and for its health benefits.

Ashoka

Ashoka is traditionally used to treat gynecological issues and is known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Shankhpushpi

Shankhpushpi is used to enhance memory, reduce anxiety, and improve mental health. It is considered a brain tonic in Ayurveda.

Guduchi

Guduchi is known for its immune-boosting, anti-inflammatory, and detoxifying properties. It is commonly used in Ayurvedic formulations.

Vacha

Vacha is used to treat respiratory conditions, improve speech, and aid digestion. It is also used to enhance cognitive function.

Haritaki

Haritaki is known for its detoxifying properties and is used in Ayurveda to support digestion and enhance longevity.

Bael

Bael is used in Ayurveda to treat digestive issues and improve immunity. It is also known for its antibacterial properties.

Kalmegh

Kalmegh is known for its immune-boosting properties and is used to treat infections and detoxify the liver.

Triphala

Triphala is a combination of three fruits (Amla, Haritaki, and Bibhitaki) used for its digestive and detoxifying properties.

Sarpagandha

Sarpagandha is traditionally used to treat hypertension and anxiety. It has calming properties.

Bhringraj

Bhringraj is used in Ayurveda to treat hair loss, improve liver function, and enhance skin health.

Yashtimadhu

Yashtimadhu, or Licorice, is used to soothe the throat, improve digestion, and boost immunity.

Moringa

Moringa is known for its high nutritional value and medicinal properties, offering anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-diabetic effects.

Giloy

Giloy is an immune-boosting herb used in Ayurveda to treat fever, diabetes, and enhance overall immunity.

Cinnamon

Cinnamon is widely used for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties, supporting digestive health and reducing blood sugar levels.

Sativa

Sativa is used for its calming effects, often used in traditional medicine for stress relief and promoting mental clarity.
