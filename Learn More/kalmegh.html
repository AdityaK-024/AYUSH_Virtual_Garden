Plant Name: Kalmegh

Scientific Name: Andrographis paniculata

Common Names: Kalmegh, Green Chiretta, King of Bitters

Origin: Native to India and Southeast Asia, Kalmegh grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

Uses in Medicine: Kalmegh is widely used in traditional medicine for its liver detoxification and immune-boosting properties. It is also known for its potent antiviral and antimicrobial effects.

Health Benefits:

  • Supports liver function and helps detoxify the liver.
  • Boosts the immune system and fights infections.
  • Has anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
  • Helps in reducing fever and treating colds and flu.
  • Promotes digestive health by easing constipation and indigestion.

Helps in These Diseases:

  • Liver disorders: Hepatitis, jaundice, and fatty liver disease.
  • Infections: Colds, flu, and respiratory infections.
  • Digestive issues: Indigestion, constipation, and bloating.
  • Inflammation: Arthritis, joint pain, and inflammation-related disorders.

Traditional and Cultural Significance: Kalmegh has been used for centuries in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine. It is highly valued for its role in detoxifying the liver and strengthening the immune system.

Preparation and Dosage: Kalmegh is usually consumed in the form of capsules, powders, or extracts. It can also be made into a tea or decoction. It is advisable to consult a healthcare provider for proper dosage and usage.

Scientific Research: Research supports Kalmegh's potential in treating liver diseases, boosting immunity, and its antiviral properties. Studies have also shown its ability to reduce inflammation and support digestive health.
